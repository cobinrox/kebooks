Scenario 1: Clash (should I push or should I merge now)


PROBLEM: Merge conflict due to Dexter's sneaky change to the remote repo

This is the typical scenario that all of us see from time-to-time, whether using a branching workflow or not. Basically, two individuals start with the same repo and branch. One individual (Dexter) makes a change on his laptop and then pushes his changes up to the server repo. Bill, in the mean time knows nothing about Dexter's change and so, when Bill tries to push his changes, there will be a merge conflict.

But, you may, think, what if Bill and Dexter are working on separate, individual branches? There will still be a problem because both of their changes need to be at some time merged into the master branch.

The solution, therefore, is for Bill to first perform a "git pull" from the remote repo before trying to even think about pushing his changes. This is the key concept that we hope you take from this book: always pull before you push. You need to make sure you get all of the changes that may have been snuck into the master copy.
