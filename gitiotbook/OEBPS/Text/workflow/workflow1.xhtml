BRANCHING WORKFLOWS

This is probably the most boring chapter, but also the most important. In today's market, you need to understand various branching workflows, whether you agree that they are a good idea or not. If you don't understand branching workflows, you may stumble when trying to understand Git.  If you are certain that you understand the nuances of a basic branching strategy, you may want to skip this chapter. But if the phrase "Did you merge your branch into the master?" seems perfectly fine to you, then you might want to browse through the sections herein.  In fact, after reading through this chapter, it is hoped that you will be able to answer the following questions (no this is not a test, but it is important):

  • How do you prevent developer X's changes breaking the changes that developer Y made last week? (answer: Developer X cannot merge into the master remote repo until they have merged the master into your their branch themselves locally on their machine and a) have unmangled any collisions all locally; AND b) make sure unit tests still work after merging master into their branch locally

This chapter brings you up to speed with the primary concept of branching workflows, with the following sub-sections:

  • The Wild West No-Branching Workflow
  • The Branching Workflow
  • The Simple but Safe Branching Workflow
  • The Branching-on-Steroids Branching Workflow
